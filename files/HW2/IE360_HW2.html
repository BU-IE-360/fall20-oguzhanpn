1. Introduction:¶

Turkey is referred as emerging market in global economy; therefore, there are many global investments in Turkey. Those foreign investments have been an indispensible part of the economy so in every economic decision foreign investors response must be considered carefully. Therefore, during an economic downfall decisions should be made accordingly. However, such decisions are generally creates very high inflation which melts downs wealth of Turkish household. Eventhough interests given to Turkish Lira deposits is very high, it cannot compansate the changes in exchange rates. In response, Turkish household prefers to keep their money in foreign exchange deposits. Which is called dollarization of Turkish Lira.

Until early 2016, we have been experiencing economically unstable times which results with dollarization of Turkish Lira. I will select some related data with dollarization. Firstly, people tend to move dollar instead of Turkish Lira because of the value loss in Turkish Lira. So I will take inflation rate of Turkish Lira as m first feature. Secondly, rise of USD/TRY exchange rate in recent years is one of the key factors for dollarization; therefore, I will include this to my analysis. Finally, while making decision between TRY and Dollar, one should be considering the interest given to those currencies in Turkish banks. Therefore, I will use those rates as my third and fourth feature. To sum up, in this study, I will analyze "Is dollarization related with monthly inflation of Turkey, USD/TRY exchange rate, and interest rate given Lira deposits and Dollar deposits in Turkey".

As the indicator of dollarizaiton I will use the ratio of foreign exchange deposits among all deposits in Turkish banks. This indicator shows the househould's preference between Turkish Lira and foreign currencies, therefore, it can be a good indicator for dollarization. As the data source I will use the database of Turkish Central Bank (https://evds2.tcmb.gov.tr/).

Now I will try to analyze movements of my features and foreign exchange deposit share by inspecting the lineplots above one by one.

1.  Monthly inflation & foreign exchange deposit share:
Monthly inflation shows the loss in the value of Turkish Lira at the month. From the plot above, we cannot observe a strong relationship between them. But it is seen that after the peak times in foreign deposit share, monthly inflation rises. The mechanism behind this might be like this increasing foreign exchange deposit share decreases the demand for Turkish Lira and therefore USD/TRY rate rises. It will result in rise in price level but this rise happens one month later since the adjustment of prices are slow. (lagged inflation ~ foreign exchange deposit share)

2. USD/TRY rate & foreign exchange deposit share:
In theory there is two-sided relationship between them beacuse increasing foreign exchange deposit share decreases the demand for Turkish Lira which rises the USD/TRY exchange rate (lagged USD/TRY ~ foreign exchange deposit share). On the other hand, increasing USD/TRY rate generally shapes the expectation of households and this makes people keep their money in USD instead of TRY (lagged foreign exchange deposit share ~ USD/TRY ). From the second graph above, we see the strong relationship between the two variables. Also the first impact is more observable from the graph.

3. TRY interest rate & foreign exchange deposit share:
The interest given to Turkish Lira should have made Turkish people keep their money in Turkish Lira; therefore, I would expect negative correlation between two. However, in the third graph above, we see co-movements of the two variables.

4. USD interest rate & foreign exchange deposit share:
We would expect the opposite of TRY interest ie. co-movement of variables and we see it from the graph.

Correlation heatmap shows us the correlations and auotocorrelations between all variables and their 3 lags. Foreign exchange deposit share has the strongest correlation with USD/TRY rate which makes sense and the reasons behind it are explained above. Interestingly second hisghest correlation is with TRY interest and its lags. We observe older interest rates (lag 3) has more impact on foreign deposit share. Interest given to USD deposits in Turkish bakns doesn't seem to be correlated with foreign exchange deposit share. But we again see the rising correlation with older variables. The correlation betweem inflation and foreign exchange deposit share is not high therefore, we can say that change in overall price level has less impact on Turkish household to turn foreign currencies compared to exchange rates etc.

3. Conclusion:¶

In this work I have analyzed dollarization of Turkey in last 7 years. For the dollarization measure, Foreign Exchange Deposit Share in Turkish banks is used. I have first shown that Foreign Exchange Deposit Share have a rising trend and have risen up to 55% nowadays which was around 32% in 2013. Then some related feauteres and their correlations with Foreign Exchange Deposit Share is analyzed. Firstly, USD/TRY is found to have the closest relationship with Foreign Exchange Deposit Share and therefore with dollarization. Second finding is, counter-intuitevly, interest given Turkish could not succeed to turn Turkish household buying dollars. The reason behind this might be the very strong rise in USD/TRY exchange rate could not be compansated by Lira interest. For interest given USD in Turkish banks and monthly inflation we found positive but weak correlations.
